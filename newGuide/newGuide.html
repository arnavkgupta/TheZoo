Spread Awareness Through Social Media
You can create artwork that showcases the beauty and importance of endangered species. You can use social media to showcase your artwork and raise awareness about endangered species. You can also share information about conservation efforts and ways that people can get involved.
Saving Endangered Species Youth Art Contest
This is an annual art competition organized by the Endangered Species Coalition, open to K-12 students in the US, with a different theme each year to create awareness on endangered species and their conservation. Winners get prizes and recognition.
Donate a Percentage of Profits to Conservation Organizations
If you sell your artwork, you can donate a percentage of the profits to organizations that work to protect endangered species. Some organizations you could donate to include the World Wildlife Fund, Wildlife Conservation Society, The Nature Conservancy, and the Rainforest Alliance.
The Endangered Species Print Project
This is an annual printmaking project that invites artists to create limited-edition prints of endangered species. The prints are sold to raise funds for conservation efforts. You can participate by submitting your artwork for consideration
The World Wildlife Fund's Annual Art Contest
WWF's annual art contest invites artists of all ages to create artwork inspired by wildlife and nature. The contest is open to artists from around the world and winners receive prizes and have their artwork showcased on WWF's website.
Run for Wildlife
This is an organization that hosts charity runs and walks to raise funds and awareness for conservation efforts. You can find events near you or participate in virtual events from anywhere in the world.
World Wildlife Fund's Panda Nation
This is a fundraising platform created by the World Wildlife Fund (WWF) that allows individuals and teams to fundraise by participating in running and other fitness events.
Defenders of Wildlife's Wildlife Run
Defenders of Wildlife hosts an annual virtual 5k called the Wildlife Run. The event raises funds for the organization's work to protect wildlife and their habitats.
ConservationFIT
This is a virtual run that raises funds for the Wildlife Conservation Society. Participants can run, walk, or bike and raise funds for the organization's conservation efforts.
Virtual Running Club
This is an online community of runners who are passionate about conservation. The club hosts virtual events and challenges to raise funds and awareness for conservation organizations.
Host a Lifting Competition for Conservation
You can organize a lifting competition and donate the proceeds to a conservation organization that works to protect endangered species. You can partner with local gyms, businesses, and organizations to make the event a success.

Read Books About Endangered Species
You can start by reading books that provide information on endangered species, their habitats, and the challenges they face. By learning more about these animals, you will be better equipped to take action and support conservation efforts.
Donate Your Used Books to the Local Library
Donating books you’ve read already reduces waste!
Join a Conservation-focused Book Club
Read and discuss books about conservation and endangered species in a book club that focuses on these topics. Here are some organizations that offer resources and book recommendations for conservation-focused book clubs: The Jane Goodall Institute, The Nature Conservancy, The Nature Conservancy.
Use Eco-friendly Instruments and Equipment
Single-use plastics, such as straws, plastic bags, and water bottles, can harm wildlife and their habitats. By reducing your use of these products, you can help reduce plastic pollution and protect endangered species. Resources: Reverb, Earthwood Guitars, Artiphon, Sweetwater.
Host a Benefit Concert or Jam Session
You can organize a benefit concert or jam session to raise awareness and funds for endangered species. Invite other musicians and encourage them to perform songs that support the cause. You can also sell merchandise or raffle items to raise additional funds.
Music Against Animal Cruelty
Get involved with this nonprofit organization that uses music to make a difference and aid in wildlife conservation.
Choose Cruelty-free Makeup
One way to help endangered species is to choose makeup brands that do not test their products on animals. Look for makeup brands that are certified as cruelty-free by organizations like PETA or Leaping Bunny.
Choose Sustainable and Eco-friendly Makeup Products
Look for makeup products that are made from sustainable and eco-friendly materials, such as bamboo or recycled materials. There are several brands that offer eco-friendly makeup products that are also vegan and cruelty-free such as: Elate Cosmetics, Aether Beauty, Kjaer Weis, Zao Organic Makeup
Participate in eco-friendly makeup challenges
There are several online makeup challenges that encourage participants to use eco-friendly makeup products or to create makeup looks inspired by endangered species. By participating in these challenges, you can raise awareness about endangered species and promote the use of eco-friendly makeup products.
How You Can Make Your Makeup Routine More Eco-friendly
Hear from Experts
Repurpose and Upcycle Clothing
Instead of buying new clothing, consider repurposing and upcycling clothing that you already own. You could turn old t-shirts into tote bags, or use fabric from old clothing to create patches for new clothing.
Buy Less or Buy Used
Reduce consumption and waste by thrifting or buying less clothing! Not only does buying used clothes reduce the number of natural resources being used, it also reduces the amount of energy used and pollution that’s being emitted.
Choose sustainable and ethical fashion
Look for brands that use eco-friendly materials, such as organic cotton or recycled fabrics, and that have fair labor practices. Here are some examples: Patagonia, Reformation, Everlane, People Tree.
Participate in an Eco-friendly Fashion Challenge
The Redress Design Award is a global sustainable fashion design competition that aims to promote sustainable fashion and reduce textile waste. Participants are challenged to create fashion designs using sustainable materials and techniques.
Support Environmentally Responsible Ski Resorts
Look for ski resorts that have a strong commitment to sustainability and conservation, such as reducing greenhouse gas emissions, using renewable energy sources, and protecting wildlife habitats. Examples: Vail Resorts, Aspen Snowmass, Jackson Hole Mountain Resort.
Avoid Single-use Plastics
Bring a reusable water bottle and avoid using single-use plastics, such as disposable cups and straws, when you're skiing or snowboarding. Single-use plastics can end up in the environment and harm wildlife, so reducing your use of them can help protect endangered species.
Choose Sustainable and Ethical Ski-wear Brands
When shopping for ski wear, look for companies that use sustainable materials, manufacturing processes, and ethical practices. You can also check for certifications.
Choose Sustainable Seafood
Many species of fish and other seafood are overfished or harvested in ways that harm other marine life. By choosing sustainable seafood, you can help protect these species and their ecosystems. Look for seafood that is certified by organizations.
Host a Bake Sale Fundraiser
Host a bake sale and donate the proceeds to an organization that works to protect endangered species and their habitats. This is a fun and tasty way to raise awareness and support a good cause.
Cook with Plant-based Ingredients
Raising animals for food can have a significant impact on the environment. By incorporating more plant-based ingredients into your cooking, you can reduce your environmental footprint and help protect endangered species and their habitats.
Support Sustainable Agriculture
Many species of plants and animals depend on healthy ecosystems for their survival. By choosing ingredients that are grown using sustainable farming practices, you can help protect ecosystems and the species that depend on them. Look for foods certified organic or grown using regenerative agriculture practices.
Reduce Food Waste
Food waste is a major environmental issue that contributes to greenhouse gas emissions and other environmental problems. By reducing your food waste, you can help protect endangered species and their habitats by reducing the demand for new agricultural land and resources.
Plant a Garden
Growing your own fruits and vegetables is not only a fun and rewarding hobby but also helps reduce your carbon footprint and support local ecosystems. You can also create a habitat for pollinators by planting native plants that attract bees, butterflies, and other beneficial insects.
Support Food Conservation Organizations
Here are some organizations that work to protect endangered species and their habitats and focus specifically on food-related issues such as sustainable agriculture and seafood: The Sustainable Food Trust, Seafood Watch, Ocean Wise, FishChoice, Environmental Defense Fund.
Respect the Environment
Stay on designated trails to avoid trampling on delicate vegetation, and avoid disturbing wildlife by keeping a safe distance and not feeding or approaching them.
Learn about Species in the Area
Research the endangered species that live in the area where you will be hiking and be aware of any specific conservation efforts that are underway to protect them.
Report Sightings
UIf you spot an endangered species while on a hike, report the sighting to the appropriate authorities or conservation organizations.
Leave No Trace
Make sure to pack out all trash and minimize your impact on the environment by following the principles of Leave No Trace.
Reduce your Carbon Footprint
Try to reduce your carbon footprint by carpooling or using public transportation to get to classes or performances. You can also reduce your environmental impact by using reusable water bottles and snack containers.
Use Sustainable Dancewear
Look for dancewear brands that use sustainable and eco-friendly materials, or consider repurposing old dance costumes to reduce waste. Here are some examples of eco-friendly brands: Wolven, Girlfriend Collective, MATE the Label, Inner FIre, K.deer.
Organize a Benefit Dance/Singing Event
If you have the resources and support, consider organizing a benefit dance/singing event to raise funds for a conservation organization or endangered species protection program.
Use Your Art as a Way to Connect with Nature
Consider taking your dance practice outdoors and using nature as inspiration for your movements. By connecting with the natural world, you may gain a deeper appreciation for the importance of protecting endangered species and their habitats.
Gather your Team for a Fun Cleanup Event
Look out for beach/environmental cleanup events near you and gather your teammates to volunteer together. Not only will it be a fun bonding experience but cleaning up the environment is a great way to protect animals.
Host a Sports Tournament Fundraiser
Organize a sports tournament at your school where students can form teams to compete against each other (for a fee). Donate the funds you gain to a conservation fund! Some ideas for tournaments include 3v3 basketball, 5v5 soccer, and capture the flag.
Buy Eco-friendly Sporting Gear
Look for sports gear made from sustainable materials or from eco-friendly businesses. By choosing products that are environmentally friendly, you can reduce your impact on wildlife habitats. There are several brands that offer eco-friendly sporting gear. Examples: Patagonia, Adidas, Bureo, Cotopaxi, EcoVessel.
